HISTORY OF FIRE PREVENTION MONTH






In 1922, the National Fire Protection Association started to commemorate the GReat Chicago Fire of 1871. This event of the month teaches everyone around the world on how to be safe and how to prevent fires. President Calcin proclaimed the first fire prevention week on 1925, october 4th-10th. The President stated , “This waste results from the conditions which justify a sense of shame and horror; for the greater part of it could and ought to be prevented… It is highly desirable that every effort be made to reform the conditions which have made possible so vast a destruction of the national wealth”.
From October 4-10, the Great Chicago fire burned and damaged the most on the 9th. $200 million dollars worth, 300 deaths, and 17 thousand buildings destroyed in the incident. A martial law was decalred soon after. The fire started due to a cow kicking a lantern in the O’Leary barn in the southwest side of the city. In 1997, the Chicago City Council made a resolution free Catherine O'Leary along with her cow. The Chicago Fire Deartment Training Academy is currently located on O'Leary's property.
